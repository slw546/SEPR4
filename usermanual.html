User Manual

If you need any help using Bear Traffic Contoller: Over the Cloud then take a look at the user manual below.

Launching the game

 

Main Menu

After launching the game, you will be presented with the following main menu.

  1. Current date and time are displayed in the top right corner of the main menu.
  2. Brief Credits of the development team.
  3. Selecting the “PLAY MULTIPLAYER” button will allow the user to host a multiplayer game or connect to an existing multiplayer host.
  4. Selecting the “PLAY GAME” button will launch the difficulty select screen for the main game, the user can start the game from here.
  5. Selecting the “CREDITS” button will show details of the developers of the game. The credits screen can be exited by pressing the “Escape Key”.
  6. Selecting the “HELP” button will open this manual in your default browser.
  7. Selecting the “EXIT” button will exit the game.

Single Player Mode

 

Difficulty Selection Interface

Upon selecting the “PLAY GAME” button on the main menu, you are presented with the following difficulty select screen.

  1. Selecting the “EASY” button will launch a single player game in easy difficulty, the aircraft here travel at a slow speed.
  2. Selecting the “MEDIUM” button will launch a single player game in medium difficulty, the aircraft here travel at a medium speed.
  3. Selecting the “HARD” button will launch a single player game in hard difficulty, the aircraft here travel at a fast speed.

The difficulty determines the speed of the aircraft, the rate at which flights spawn and the separation violation distance. This is the distance in which planes have to be from each other, breaking this rule will lead to a loss of points.

You can exit to the main menu by pressing the “Escape” key.

 

Single Player Interface

After selecting a difficulty you will be presented with the following interface.

  1. The main airspace, this displays aircraft, the waypoints and the the route in which the aircraft is taking.
  2. This box contains information about the selected aircraft.
  3. The altimeter, this displays the selected aircraft’s altitude and allows you to change it.
  4. The communication log, this box will constantly update displaying messages between the air traffic controller and the pilots of the aircraft. This will warn you when aircraft will be arriving at a waypoint.

 

1. Main Airspace Interface

  1. Plane Counter, this displays the number of aircraft in the airspace.
  2. Timer, this displays the elapsed time since the game began.
  3. Score/Earnings, this displays the current amount of money you have earnt.
  4. Origin/Destination Waypoint, aircraft will enter/leave the airspace via these waypoints.
  5. Plane, this is the entity that you need to maneuver around the airspace.
  6. Waypoint, these are used to control the route the aircraft will take around the airspace.
  7. Landing Waypoint, there are two of these for each airport, these are the final waypoints of an aircraft’s flight plan, aircraft will circle here waiting for clearance to land.
  8. Airport, one of two airports in the airspace that an aircraft can take-off or land in.
  9. Terminal, aircraft that have landed at the airport will taxi to these parking points before being allowed to take-off again.
  10. Selected Aircraft, this aircraft has been selected, you can see further detail of a selected aircraft below.

1.J Selected Aircraft

  1. Plane Bearing, this large green circle denotes the bearing around the aircraft that it can yaw to (in degrees). The green pointer protruding from the aircraft denotes the current bearing, as well as allowing the user to adjust the bearing.
  2. Separation Violation Warning, the red circle will surround two aircraft that are at risk of colliding, highlighting a separation violation. The radius that aircraft have to be separated to avoid the points penalty depends on the difficulty setting selected.
  3. Current Altitude, all aircraft display their current altitude next to their icon.
  4. Flight Path, this is the route which the aircraft is traveling along, this can be interacted with to change the route the aircraft will take to it’s destination.

 

2. Flight Display

  1. Flight Code, this is a unique number identifying each flight.
  2. Altitude, this is the aircraft’s current altitude.
  3. Speed, this is the aircraft’s current speed.
  4. Origin, this is the entry/exit waypoint the aircraft entered the airspace via.
  5. Destination, this is the entry/exit waypoint the aircraft should exit the airspace via, this includes landing at airports.
  6. Value, this is the amount of points you earn for carrying that aircraft to its exit waypoint.

 

3. Altitude Display

  1. Up Arrow, clicking this button will increase the selected aircraft’s altitude if not already at maximum.
  2. Down Arrow, clicking this will decrease the selected aircraft’s altitude if not already at minimum.
  3. Current Altitude, displays the current altitude of the selected aircraft.
  4. Altimeter, displays the altitude, with the alignment of each altitude division, and the horizon line of the plane denoting it’s current altitude.
  5. Roll Display, displays the current roll/level if the aircraft. Useful to display the direction an aircraft is turning.

 

4. Communication Log

  1. Incoming Message, (<<<) this denotes information created by the default operations of the aircraft.
  2. Outgoing Message, (>>>) this denotes the information relevant to your intervention on the aircraft’s route.

 

Shop/Game Success Screen

The above screen is shown upon successful completion of the game, it ultimately decides the outcome of your bear’s story.

  1. MONEY, The money you earned during the game (displayed in Rubles).
  2. Items: Clicking these items will purchase them, spending your hard earned Rubles and ultimately deciding the fate of you and your cubs.

 

Playing Singleplayer

Objective

The primary objective of Bear Traffic Control is to earn the maximum amount of money possible by instructing planes so as they travel safely through the airspace, along their intended path between point of origin and destination.

To win the game, regardless of money earned, you must successfully guide all planes around the airspace, between its the defined origin/destination points of the plane

Money

  • Each plane starts off with a value of 100 Rubles (R100).
  • Poor/careless manoeuvring of planes will result in deductions from a plane’s value:
    • A deduction of between R10-R1 for separation violations.
    • A deduction of R2 or R1 for each time the flight plan is altered (i.e., the path changed to use different waypoints).
  • The value of a plane is banked upon it successfully leaving the airspace (having visited the airport if necessary).
    • Banking is where the value of a plane is added to the total, cumulative score/amount earned.

Money can be exchanged for goods and services. A variety of goods are available to purchase in the in-game shop.

Flights

  • Each plane will originate from one of the four corners of the map, Moscow, Khorlovo, Peski, or Malino (Clockwise from top-right/North-West, 1.d/2.d).
    • These four points also act as destinations, along with Chkalovsky Airport (1.h).
  • The plane will automatically be given a destination (2.e) and a flightpath (1.j.IV) via any assortment of waypoints (1.f) to travel via to reach that destination.
  • The flight will automatically fly along the flightpath, taking the straightest, most direct route permitted by its turn radius, unless the user intervenes.
    • Altitude (1.j.III, 2.b, 3) will remain fixed unless altered by the player.
  • The destination will be one of two categories:
    • One of the origin points, in which case the plane will depart the airspace having reached such point (completed flight).
    • A landing point (1.g), a point that must be passed, in order the plane can subsequently land at the airport (1.h).
      • In order for a plane to land having passed the landing point, the player must instruct it to do so (i.e., a plane will not land automatically).
      • The plane will circle its assigned landing point until the player clears it to land.
  • If the destination is a landing point, upon reaching the terminal and parking (1.i), the player must then clear the aircraft to take off.
    • Upon take off, the plane will be given a new destination and flightpath (and value).
    • Having taken off, it will then behave as any plane with a destination from (1.d), designated automatically and listed at (2.d).

Multiplayer Mode

Bear Traffic Control supports a two player multiplayer mode which allows two players to play against each other on a local area network.

 

Multiplayer Menu Interface

When the multiplayer button on the main menu is selected, the multiplayer menu is displayed. The escape key can be pressed to return to the main menu.

  1. Host LAN Game, this button will host a local game at your ip address using the default port 4444. The escape key can be pressed to stop waiting for a client and return to the main menu.
  2. IP Address Box, entering an IP address here with the ip of the host you wish to connect to.
  3. Start Client, this button will connect to the IP address entered into the IP Address Box, the game will start when the host selects the Start Game button.
  4. Start Game, this button is only visible to the host and when a client has connected, it allows the host to start the game when both players are ready.

 

Multiplayer Interface

When the host selects the “Start Game” button, both players are presented with the main multiplayer interface. This is almost identical to the single player interface, however there are some important differences.

  1. Separating Line, this line divides the host’s airspace from the client’s airspace. The host can only select and control aircraft and flight plans on the left side of this line, and the client can only select and control aircraft and flight plans on the right side of this line.
  2. Multiplayer Score, this displays your current score on the left and your opponents current score on the right.

 

Playing Multiplayer

Objective

Unlike single player, you aren’t playing until the first collision between two aircraft, instead you will be playing against an opponent to expand your territory to cover the entire airspace. To expand your half of the airspace you must accumulate more points than your opponent, doing this will shift the dividing line between towards your opponents side.

Controls

 

Select a Plane

To select a plane, simply left-click the plane. This will select it, update the Flight Display (2), and display the plane overlay (1.j). N.B. Planes are not selectable upon approach for landing and during take-off. When selecting a plane parked at the terminal, the plane overlay will not display (as you cannot directly control it), however the Flight Display will still update.

Yaw a Plane

To alter a selected plane’s bearing, either left click any area within the bearing ring (1.j.I) to make the plane turn to face that bearing/direction, or use the arrow keys ←/→ to turn anti-clockwise (nose turns left, relatively) and clockwise (nose turns right, relatively) respectively.

Alter Altitude

To change the altitude of the plane that is selected, either click 3.a or 3.b to increase/decrease altitude respectively, or by pressing the arrow keys ↑/↓ to increase/decrease.

Change Flightpath

To modify the flightpath of a plane, with the plane selected, click and drag a waypoint 1.f that is along the flightpath 1.j.IV, releasing when the (darker) blue line is over another waypoint. Beware that doing this will incur a penalty as detailed in the money section previously, mostly due to the nausea caused to the passengers as the plane rolls to-and-fro while you zig and zag in your bewildered incompetence.

Landing

To clear the currently selected plane to land, simply press the L key once it has passed through its landing point 1.g. Once you have cleared it to land, you will relinquish manual control, as it automatically approaches the runway (going offscreen if required). It will remain unselectable until it reaches and parks at the terminal. Once it has reached the terminal it is immediately selectable, due to an elaborate scam whereby passengers can be charged twice if they don’t have time to disembark.

Take-Off

To take off, first select a parked plane, then press the T key. As with landing, the plane will deselect and cannot be selected until it has finished taking off and is at cruising altitude, at which point it is safe to use your in-flight electronics and refreshments will be served alongside a light lunch.
